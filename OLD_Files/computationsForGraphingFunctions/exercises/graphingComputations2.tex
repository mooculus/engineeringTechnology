\begin{exercise}

Consider the function $f$ given by
\[
f(x) = \frac{48}{12+(x-1)^2}.
\]

The domain of $f$ is $\left(\answer{-\infty},\answer{\infty}\right)$.

$f$ has a single horizontal asymptote at $y=\answer{0}$.

$f$ has $\answer{0}$ vertical asymptotes.

The derivative of $f$ is
\[
f'(x) =\frac{ -96(\answer{x-1})}{(12+(x-1)^2)^2}.
\]

The second derivative of $f$ is
\[
f''(x) = \frac{288(x-3)(\answer{x+1})}{(12+(x-1)^2)^3}.
\]

$f$ is increasing and concave down on $\left(\answer{-1},\answer{1}\right)$.

$f$ is increasing and concave up on $\left(\answer{-\infty},\answer{-1}\right)$.

$f$ is decreasing and concave down on $\left(\answer{1},\answer{3}\right)$.

$f$ is decreasing and concave up on $\left(\answer{3},\answer{\infty}\right)$.

$f$ has $\answer{0}$ local minima.

$f$ has 1 local maximum at $x=\answer{1}$.

$f$ has inflection points (from left to right) at $x=\answer{-1}$ and $x=\answer{3}$.
