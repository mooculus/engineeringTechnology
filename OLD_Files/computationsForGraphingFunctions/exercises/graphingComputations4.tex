\begin{exercise}

Consider the function $f(x)$ with derivative
\[
f'(x) = (x-2)^3.
\]

$f$ is increasing on the interval $\left(\answer{2},\answer{\infty}\right)$.

$f$ has no local \wordChoice{\choice{minima}\choice[correct]{maxima}}.

$f$ has a local \wordChoice{\choice[correct]{minimum}\choice{maximum}} at $x=\answer{2}$.

The second derivative of $f$ is given by
\[
f''(x) = \answer{3(x-2)^2}.
\]

$f$ is concave \wordChoice{\choice[correct]{up}\choice{down}} on the interval $\left(-\infty,\answer{\infty}\right)$.

$f$ \wordChoice{\choice{has some}\choice[correct]{does not have any}} inflection points.

\end{exercise}
