\begin{exercise}

Consider the differential equation $(4x-1)y'+4y=0$

Write the equation in the form $y' = f(x,y)$, we find:

\[
y' = \answer{-\frac{4y}{4x-1}}
\]

If $y(x)$ is a solution to this differential equation, we expect that the solution will have a vertical tangent line at $x=\answer{\frac{1}{4}}$.

\begin{exercise}
The direction field for this differential equation is shown below:

\begin{image}
{\def\length{sqrt(1+((4*y)/(4*x-1))^2)}
\begin{tikzpicture}
  \begin{axis}[
      xmin=-2, xmax=2,ymin=-3,ymax=3,domain=-3:3,view={0}{90},
      axis lines =center, xlabel=$x$, ylabel=$y$,
      every axis y label/.style={at=(current axis.above origin),anchor=south},
      every axis x label/.style={at=(current axis.right of origin),anchor=west},
      axis on top,
    ] 
    \addplot3 [penColor, quiver={u={1/\length}, v={(4*y)/(4*x-1)/(\length)},scale arrows=.2},samples=36] {0};
]  \end{axis}
\end{tikzpicture}}
\end{image}

Does it appear that the solutions have a vertical tangent line at $x=\frac{1}{4}$?

\begin{multipleChoice}
\choice[correct]{Yes}
\choice{No}
\end{multipleChoice}
\end{exercise}
\end{exercise}
