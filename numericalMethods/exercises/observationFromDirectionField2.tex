\begin{exercise}

The slope field for the differential equation $y' = \frac{x^2e^{xy}}{y}$ is shown below:

\begin{image}
{\def\length{sqrt(1+((x^2*exp(x*y))/(y))^2)}
\begin{tikzpicture}
  \begin{axis}[
      xmin=-5, xmax=5,ymin=-3,ymax=5.2,domain=-5:5.2,view={0}{90},
      axis lines =center, xlabel=$x$, ylabel=$y$,
      every axis y label/.style={at=(current axis.above origin),anchor=south},
      every axis x label/.style={at=(current axis.right of origin),anchor=west},
      axis on top,
    ] 
    \addplot3 [penColor, quiver={u={1/\length}, v={(x^2*exp(x*y))/(y)/(\length)},scale arrows=.2},samples=30] {0};
]  \end{axis}
\end{tikzpicture}}
\end{image}

Now, consider the initial value problem:

\[
\begin{cases}
~ y' &=  \frac{x^2e^{xy}}{y} \\
 ~ y(0) &= 2
\end{cases}
\]

From the direction field, it looks like the solution has a vertical asymptote at $x=\answer[tolerance=.2]{1}$.

\end{exercise}
