The graph of one complete period of a cosine curve is given below.  We are told that the period is 12.


\begin{image}
  \begin{tikzpicture}
    \begin{axis}[
        xmin=-2.3,xmax=10.3,ymin=-3.3,ymax=3.3,
        clip=false,
        unit vector ratio*=1 1 1,
        axis lines=center,
        grid = major,
        ytick={-3,-2,...,3},
    xtick={0},
        xlabel=$x$, ylabel=$y$,
        every axis y label/.style={at=(current axis.above origin),anchor=south},
        every axis x label/.style={at=(current axis.right of origin),anchor=west},
      ]
      \addplot[very thick,penColor,domain = -2:10,samples=50] plot{2*cos(pi/6*(x+2)*180/pi)};
      
      \addplot[black,only marks,mark=*] coordinates{(-2,2) (1,0) (4,-2) (10,2)};
      \node at (axis cs:-2,2) [black,above] {$({-2},2)$};
      \node at (axis cs:1,0) [black,above right] {$A$};
      \node at (axis cs:4,-2) [black,above] {$B$};
      \node at (axis cs:10,2) [black,above] {$C$};
      \end{axis}`
  \end{tikzpicture}
\end{image}

An equation for this curve is given by
\[
y = \answer{2}\cos\left(\answer{\frac{\pi}{6}}(x+2)\right).
\]

Fill in the blanks below.
\begin{align*}
A &= \left(\answer{1},0\right)\\
B &= \left(\answer{4},-2\right)\\
C &= \left(\answer{10},2\right)
\end{align*}

\end{exercise}
